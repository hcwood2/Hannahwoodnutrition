You’re not imagining it: Women are under‑supported

If you’ve ever sat across from a doctor, trying to explain that something just doesn’t feel right — and left with a handout, a birth control script, or worse, absolutely nothing — you’re not alone. And you’re not imagining it.

Women are consistently under‑cared for in the health system. Not just occasionally. Systemically.

We have a long history of telling women to be quieter about their symptoms. To tough it out. To keep pushing. To lose weight. To meditate. To “circle back if it gets worse.” Meanwhile, many of the conditions that impact women the most — PCOS, thyroid disease, menopause — are underfunded, under‑researched and wildly misunderstood.

We call it “women’s health,” but rarely do we treat it with the urgency, nuance or respect it deserves. And that has consequences.

The longer hormone imbalances go untreated, the more likely they are to impact digestion, metabolism, sleep, energy, mood and long‑term disease risk. You don’t just wake up with chronic pain or panic attacks overnight — these are symptoms that evolve when the early signs are brushed aside.

But you deserve better. You deserve someone who listens without assuming. Someone who knows that your story matters more than a lab range. Someone who sees the whole picture, not just the piece you had time to explain in a 7‑minute visit.
